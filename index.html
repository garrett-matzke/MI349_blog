Published on September 20, 2025 by Garrett Matzke

Arificial Turf in Football

Published on September 25, 2025 by Garrett Matzke

Artifical turf in college and pro football is becoming more common because of the low maintenece. The downside is the amount of injuries that come from artifial turf because of it not being natural. It is widely known all football players would much rather play on real grass than turf.

The amount of non contact injuries that occur on turf compared to grass is quite large. When I say non contact I mean the player is injured without coming in contact with another player. The most common amd possibly the worst injuries are non contact such as ACL, MCL and achillies injuries which are all very detrimental to a player and there longevity.

I think they really need to look at the numbers on how many players have non contact injuries on turf compared to real grass. I have personally watched many players end there season early due to a non contact injury like an ACL tear on turf and depending on the player it could ruin a teams season hopes of going to the playoffs. One example is Metlife statdium in New York and the maount of non contact injuries on that field is very high and is known as one of the most dangerous fields to play on.
